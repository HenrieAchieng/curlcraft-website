Know Your Texture
Understanding African Hair Types

Your hair type is the foundation of everything — knowing it means better products, better routines, and healthier hair.

Type 3C
Tight Curls

Dense, springy corkscrew curls with lots of volume. Prone to dryness — needs moisture-first products and gentle detangling.

Type 4A
Coily S-Pattern

Defined S-shaped coils with very high moisture needs. Shrinks a lot but stretches beautifully. Thrives with the LOC method.

Type 4B
Z-Pattern Coils

Sharp Z-shaped bends with less defined pattern. High shrinkage, very fragile at the bends — sealing in moisture is key.

Type 4C
Tight Zigzag

The tightest coil pattern, most common in African hair. Enormous shrinkage, densest texture — the crown of crowns. Requires deep conditioning weekly.

Build Your Healthy Hair Routine

A consistent routine is the single biggest thing you can do for your hair. Here's the CurlCraft foundational weekly routine for 4A–4C hair.

Get a Personalised Routine
1

Pre-poo with flaxseed oil

Apply our Scalp Nourishment flaxseed oil Blend to detangle your hair 10-20 mins before washing. This helps to soften the hair and make it easier to detangle.Rinse out after 10-20 mins

2

shampoo

Use our Shampoo to cleanse your hair and remove buildup.Massage gently to open the cuticles. Scalp massage is your best friend — it stimulates growth and removes buildup without breakage.

3

Deep condition

Apply the Shea Butter conditioner from root to tip. Sit under a hooded dryer or wrap in a warm towel for 30 minutes. This is non-negotiable for type 4 hair.

4

LOC or LCO method

Seal in all that moisture: Liquid (water or aloe mist), our Leave-In Cream, then a sealing Oil. This is what keeps 4C hair moisturised all week.

5

Protect & sleep right

Satin bonnet or pillowcase every single night. Pineapple your hair or do a loose twist-out to protect your curl pattern while you sleep.
